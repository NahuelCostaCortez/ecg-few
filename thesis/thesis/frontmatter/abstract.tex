\markboth{Abstract}{Abstract}

This Bachelor's Thesis studies electrocardiogram analysis when the ECG is treated as an image and only a small number of labeled examples is available. The practical goal is not autonomous diagnosis. The intended use is clinical triage support: identifying traces that should be reviewed earlier by a specialist while never excluding a patient automatically.

The work combines two domains. The first one is a controlled QRS/ST morphology simulator that renders V1, V2 and V3 images and assigns labels from observable rules. The second one is the Brugada HUCA dataset, processed from real clinical recordings into right precordial lead windows with a binary target: confirmed Brugada case versus control. Evaluation is patient-level leave-one-out cross-validation with reduced budgets \(K=\{2,4,8,16,32\}\) and three seeds per budget.

The final convolutional model is a ResNet18 binary classifier with supervised training and internal threshold selection. On the simulator, performance improves consistently with larger \(K\): mean balanced accuracy rises from 0.625 at \(K=2\) to 0.848 at \(K=32\), with sensitivity 0.883, specificity 0.812, F1 0.673 and ROC AUC 0.857. These results show that the synthetic task contains learnable visual signal and that the few-shot protocol works in a controlled domain.

On Brugada HUCA the behavior is much more limited. The best baseline balanced accuracy is obtained at \(K=16\), 0.516, while \(K=32\) reaches 0.499. Sensitivity remains moderate, between 0.644 and 0.730 depending on \(K\), but specificity is low, between 0.289 and 0.375. This pattern is compatible with an early warning prototype, but it is not sufficient for clinical diagnosis or autonomous screening.

A domain adaptation branch is also reviewed and executed. The tested methods are self-supervised learning, CORAL, MMD and DANN, evaluated at \(K=16\) and \(K=32\). The best real-data result is obtained by MMD at \(K=32\), with balanced accuracy 0.549, F1 0.498, sensitivity 0.649 and specificity 0.448. The improvement is measurable but modest, which points to a relevant remaining domain gap between synthetic renderings and real ECG images.

Open vision-language models are left as documented future work. The thesis describes the planned in-context learning pipeline, JSON validation and CNN comparison, but it does not report VLM metrics because complete audited inference artifacts are not yet available. The finished contribution is therefore a reproducible package for simulation, CNN training, domain adaptation, metrics, plots and cautious clinical-triage conclusions.
